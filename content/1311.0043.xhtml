Abstract
Let X be a smooth, projective and geometrically connected curve of genus at least two, defined over a number field. In 1984, Szpiro conjectured that X has a " small point ". X is a cyclic cover of prime degree of the projective line, then X has infinitely many " small points ".

Introduction1

Let X be a smooth, projective and geometrically connected curve of genus at least two, defined over a number field. In 1984, Szpiro [] conjectured that X has a " small point ", where a " small point " is an algebraic point of the curve X with " height " bounded from above in a certain way. We refer to Section 2 for a precise formulation of Szpiro 's small points conjecture. Szpiro proved that his conjecture implies an " effective Mordell conjecture ". In other words, Szpiro 's remarkable approach shows that to bound the height of all rational points of any curve X, it suffices to produce for any curve X at least one " small point ".
